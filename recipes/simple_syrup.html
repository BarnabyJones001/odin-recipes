Simple Syrup

In my culinary repertoire, there exists a humble yet indispensable recipe for simple syrup, a versatile elixir that elevates countless beverages and desserts with its effortless charm. Crafted from just two ingredients—equal parts sugar and water—this syrup embodies simplicity itself, yet its transformative power is undeniable. In a saucepan, sugar crystals dissolve gracefully into simmering water, yielding a clear, viscous liquid that sparkles with potential. Once cooled, the syrup becomes a blank canvas, ready to infuse cocktails with a whisper of sweetness or to drizzle over warm pastries, imparting a delicate glaze that glistens in the light. Its versatility knows no bounds; whether mixed into iced tea on a sweltering afternoon or brushed over freshly baked cakes to enhance their moistness, this simple syrup is a steadfast companion in my culinary adventures, adding a touch of sweetness to life's every moment.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup white sugar
  • 1 cup water

Steps

  1. Combine sugar and water in a medium saucepan over medium heat; bring to a boil, stirring evenly until sugar has disolved. Allow to cool.
  2. Pour into a clean jar and seal with a tight-fitting lid; store in the fridge for up to 1 month.
