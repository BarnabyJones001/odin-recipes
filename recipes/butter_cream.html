Butter Cream Frosting

Within my culinary repertoire lies a cherished recipe for buttercream frosting, a velvety confection that crowns cakes and cupcakes with unparalleled decadence. At its heart lies the marriage of softened butter and confectioners' sugar, whipped together until airy and light. This creamy base becomes a canvas for creativity, infused with pure vanilla extract to lend a timeless elegance, or enriched with cocoa powder for a decadent chocolate twist. With each beat of the mixer, the frosting takes on a silky texture, its pale hue transforming into a cloud-like swirl of sweetness. Spread generously atop a freshly baked cake, it becomes a work of art, its smooth peaks inviting indulgence with every bite. Whether adorning a birthday cake or delicately piped onto petit fours, this buttercream frosting is a testament to the simple joys found in a well-crafted dessert, a celebration of sweetness that lingers on the palate and in the memory long after the last crumb has been savored.

Ingredients

  • 5 TBsp salted butter
  • 5 TBsp shortening
  • 3/4 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 cups powdered sugar
  • 2 TBsp milk

Steps

  1. Combine butter and shortening and beat until light and fluffy. The mixture should be noticeably lighter in color.
  2. Beat in vanilla.
  3. Add sugar one cup at a time, waiting for each cup to be fully incorporated before adding more.
  4. Add milk one TBsp at a time until fully combined.
