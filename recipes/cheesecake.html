New York Style Cheesecake

Description

For years, I've perfected my cherished cheesecake recipe, a culinary masterpiece that never fails to dazzle taste buds and warm hearts. The foundation is a buttery graham cracker crust, meticulously pressed into the pan to provide the perfect crunchy contrast to the creamy filling. The filling itself is a harmonious blend of velvety cream cheese, farm-fresh eggs, and just the right amount of sugar, all whipped together until smooth and luscious. A splash of pure vanilla extract adds depth to the flavor profile, while a hint of lemon zest lends a subtle brightness. Baked to golden perfection, this cheesecake emerges from the oven with a tantalizing aroma that fills the kitchen with anticipation. After a cooling period, it's topped with a generous dollop of homemade whipped cream and a scatter of fresh berries, elevating each forkful to a heavenly symphony of sweet indulgence. With every slice, this cheesecake recipe embodies the essence of comfort and celebration, a timeless delight cherished by all who taste it.

Ingredients

Graham Crust

  • 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/3 cup butter, melted
  • 1/4 cup white sugar

Cheesecake

  • 3 (8oz) packages of cream cheese, room temperature
  • 1 cup white sugar
  • 4 lg eggs, room temperature
  • 1 TBsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tsp cream of tartar

Sour Cream Topping

  • 1 pint sour cream
  • 1/2 cup white sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F.
  2. Make crust: Mix graham crackers, melted butter, and sugar in a medium bowl until moistened. Press into the bottom and 1/2 inch up the sides of a 9-inch springform pan, and set aside.
  3. Make cheesecake: Mix cream cheese and sugar in a large bowl until smooth. Blend in eggs, vanilla, and cream of tartar. Pour over crust in pan.
  4. Bake in the preheated oven until center is set, about 50 minutes. Allow to cool for 5 minutes after removing from the oven.
  5. Make topping: Mix sour cream, sugar, and vanilla until smooth.
  6. Pour topping over cheescake, starting from the sides of the pan and working your way to the center.
  7. Return the cheesecake to the oven until topping is set, about 5 minutes. Allow to cool in the pan to room temperature. Chill the in the fridge for at least 6 hours before serving.
